    \tablenotetext{j}{
        The ''\textit{Type\_SB}'' parameter used in our ''super-deblending'' photometry processes, as described in Section \ref{Sec.3.2.2}. 
        \textit{Type\_SB}$=1$ represents a pure SB galaxy with SFR at least 0.6 dex above the emperical MS' SFR at its redshift and stellar mass. 
        \textit{Type\_SB}$=-1$ represents a pure MS galaxy with SFR at most 0.4 dex above the emperical MS' SFR at its redshift and stellar mass. 
        \textit{Type\_SB}$=0$ represents a galaxy with SFR in between the above two limits, or are too uncertain to be determined during our processes. 
    }
    \tablenotetext{k}{

        \textit{Type\_RLAGN}$=1$ indicates that the galaxy contains a radio loud active galactic nucleus, and its radio flux is above the $3\sigma$ of the mean radio-FIR correlation. 
        \textit{Type\_RLAGN}$=0$ is for the rest galaxies.
